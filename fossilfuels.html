Fossil Fuels

What are Fossil Fuels?

  • Some examples of fossil fuels are coal, coal products, natural gas, derived gas, crude oil, petroleum products, and non-renewable wastes.
  • Fossil fuels are made of decomposing plants and animals. These fuels can be found in the earth’s crust and contain carbon and hydrogen, both of which can be burnt for energy
  • The three different kinds of fossil fuels are Coal, Natural Gas, and Oil.
  • 4 main types are: Petroleum, coal, natural gas and orimulsion>
  • We use petroleum products to propel vehicles, heat buildings, and to produce electricity. Coal is used to generate electricity. Orimulsion is currently used as a commercial boiler fuel in power plants worldwide. We use gas in stoves.

How are Fossil Fuels affecting our Environment?

  • The parts of the world that are most affected are: Oman, Qatar, Kuwait, Saudi Arabia, Brunei Darussalam
  • When fossil fuels are burned, they release large amounts of carbon dioxide, a greenhouse gas, into the air. Greenhouse gasses trap heat in our atmosphere, causing global warming.
  • These countries are dependent on Fossil Fuels, so they burn a lot of fossil fuels which release carbon dioxide, a greenhouse gas, into the air.

Renewable and Non-Renewable Energy

  • Fossil fuels such as petroleum, coal, natural gas, and Orimulsion are called non-renewable energy. Renewable energy is energy that is collected from renewable resources that are naturally replenished on a human timescale. It includes sources such as sunlight, wind, rain, tides, waves, and geothermal heat. Although most renewable energy sources are sustainable, some are not.
  • Non-renewable energy comes from sources that will run out or will not be replenished in our lifetimes—or even in many, many lifetimes. Renewable energy creates 5x more jobs than fossil fuels.
  • Non-renewable energy comes from sources that will run out or will not be replenished in our lifetimes—or even in many, many lifetimes. (Source: National Geographic) Renewable energy creates 5x more jobs than fossil fuels.

  • Fossil fuels can be found very easily but are finite, whereas renewable energy is infinite.
  • Fossil fuels are very easy to find and are less expensive than renewable energy, so people use them more, causing our supply of them to decrease, whereas renewable energy is finite so all people have to do is purchase a solar panel one time and energy will come to them.
  • Fossil Fuels are dangerous to produce and can cause ocean oil pollution.

Greenhouse Effect - when carbon dioxide, methane, and other gasses trap heat energy and maintain Earth’s temperature range.
